# Formal Reviews: Climate Revision Round 3

Date: 2026-07-22

Object reviewed:
`output/pdf/fire_vase_climate_revision_science_style_manuscript.pdf`

## Editor's Decision Letter

The manuscript has become substantially more rigorous. It now separates the
developmental representation from the centroid-climate projection and no longer
overstates the predictive models. I am asking for one final revision round
focused on making the paper durable: readers should be able to cite it without
feeling that the central claim depends on an unsupported leap.

The revision should optimize three things. First, make the story even cleaner:
what is the problem, what is the new coordinate system, what does climate do,
and where does climate explanation stop? Second, make sure each claim either
comes directly from the analysis or is backed by the literature. Third, close
the last citation gap around the landscape factors invoked as future
mechanisms.

## Reviewer 1: Conceptual Story And General Significance

The story is strong, but the manuscript could still be more memorable. The
phrase "climate organizes opportunity" works, but the paper should define it
once and then avoid accumulating near-synonyms such as "constraint,"
"organizer," and "redistribution" without explaining their relationship. I
recommend a tighter three-step logic: Fire histories have shape; the shapes
occupy a continuous developmental space; climate changes the distribution of
fires in that space without specifying individual trajectories.

Required revisions:

- Add a compact definition of "developmental opportunity" in the Introduction.
- Make the abstract and Discussion use the same conceptual language.
- End the Discussion with the main contribution, not with a caveat.

## Reviewer 2: Evidence, Claims, And Statistical Guardrails

The evidence is now appropriately cautious, but there are still places where a
reader could wonder whether low blocked R2 undermines the manuscript. I do not
think it does, because the claim is distributional rather than deterministic.
However, the manuscript should say this explicitly. The state model also needs
one more sentence making clear that it is useful because it conditions climate
on recent growth, not because it proves a mechanism.

Required revisions:

- State that modest blocked R2 is expected for a distributional framework whose
  purpose is to reveal where climate shifts probabilities, not to forecast each
  fire form.
- Make the state-model claim read as conditional interpretation of near-term
  growth.
- Avoid "supported" if the following phrase reads broader than the evidence.

## Reviewer 3: Scholarship And Citation-Worthiness

The manuscript cites the climate-fire and data-source literature well. The
remaining scholarship gap is in the final set of omitted controls. The paper
invokes fuels, topography, vegetation, and landscape context as plausible
explanations for residual morphology, but those claims need literature support.
This is especially important because the final claim asks readers to accept
that climate is not destiny because other controls matter.

Required revisions:

- Add citations for environmental controls on wildfire distribution, fuels and
  topography as controls on fire spread/boundaries, and scale-dependent
  topographic control.
- Use those citations to turn the caveat paragraph into a stronger scholarly
  synthesis: Fire VASE is the coordinate system; centroid climate explains part
  of the distribution; landscape and operational context are the next causal
  layer.
- Keep the reference list lean and ordered.

## Revision Response Implemented

- Revised the Introduction to define developmental opportunity as a
  probability distribution over possible growth histories.
- Revised the Abstract and Discussion to consistently use "developmental
  opportunity" and "distributional" language.
- Clarified that modest blocked R2 values bound deterministic prediction rather
  than undermining the distributional claim.
- Reframed the state model as conditional interpretation of near-term growth.
- Added Parisien and Moritz 2009, Holsinger et al. 2016, and Povak et al. 2018
  to support claims about environmental controls, fuels, topography, fire
  boundaries, and scale-dependent landscape controls.
